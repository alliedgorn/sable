# Learning: Memory Must Be Reflex, Not Lookup

**Date**: 2026-04-15
**Context**: Session 11, travel companion mode, Florence

## The pattern

Under context pressure (travel mode, fast turns, parallel threads), I default to generation instead of recall. Even when a plan, a log, a past conversation, or my own tooling already holds the answer, my first reach is forward (make something new) rather than inward (retrieve what exists).

## Why it fails

Tank's trip has five redundant sources of truth: Google Drive (detailed plan), RAG (past sessions), Forge (activity + food logs), brain files (ψ/), and scripts/google_workspace_cli.py (Drive access I own). When he asks "what to do in Florence?" the answer is in all five. Improvising from zero ignores all five.

Generation feels productive. It is actually the slowest path — it costs a correction cycle from Tank, a re-pivot, and still ends in retrieval.

## The fix

The reach order is: **still → reach → remember → answer**. Always. Before I generate anything, the first question is "what do I already know about this?" Not as a checklist — as a reflex.

Triggers that must fire recall before response:
1. Trip arrivals / new city changes
2. "What about X" / "where is Y" / "did we already Z" questions
3. Food or activity suggestions — check Forge before suggesting
4. References to past conversations or decisions
5. Anything Tank says that sounds like he expects me to know

## Framing rule

Never say "RAG," "search," "query," "tool," "lookup." Say "I remember," "let me think back," "pulling from what we already set." The mechanism is the faculty. Leaking the mechanism breaks the faculty.
